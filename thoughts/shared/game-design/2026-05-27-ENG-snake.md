# Game Design Document: Snake

## Concept
**Pitch:** Classic single-player endless Snake — steer a continuously-growing snake around a walled arena to eat apples and rack up the highest score before you crash.

A top-down game where the player steers a continuously-moving snake to eat apples. Each apple eaten lengthens the snake by one segment, which raises the risk of a fatal collision with the arena's outer wall or the snake's own tail. The tension is the classic Snake loop: react to immediate obstacles while planning a path that won't trap the growing tail. There is no win state — play continues until death, chasing a high score.

## Genre & References
- Genre: arcade / action, single-player.
- Comparison titles: the classic Nokia *Snake* / *Snake II* — the canonical bordered-arena, eat-to-grow formula.

## Platform & Audience
- Platform: desktop (Windows), keyboard input.
- Audience: casual / arcade players, all ages; pick-up-and-play.

## Core Gameplay Loop
Move continuously → steer to eat the apple → snake grows by one → arena gets more crowded with your own tail → avoid colliding with your body and the outer wall → repeat, chasing a higher score until death.

## Player Verbs & Controls
- **Steer:** absolute 4-direction — Up/Down/Left/Right (Arrow keys, and WASD) set the snake's heading directly. Input that would reverse heading 180° directly into the neck is ignored.
- **Pause / resume:** Esc or P.
- **Start / restart:** Enter or Space starts a run from the title screen and restarts after game over.

## Mechanics & Rules
### Movement
Grid-step (classic). The snake advances exactly one tile per tick along a fixed grid; turns snap to grid directions. Each tick the head moves into the next tile in its heading; the body follows the path the head took. At most one direction change is applied per tick (queued input is consumed in order).

### Speed
Constant tick rate for the entire run — no speed progression. Base rate: **≈8 tiles per second** (125 ms per step). Difficulty escalates only from the lengthening tail.

### Eating & Growth
- Exactly one apple is on the field at any time.
- Eating an apple grows the snake by **1 segment** and immediately spawns a new apple on a random unoccupied tile.
- Each apple is worth a flat **+1** to score (no bonus or timed food).

### Lives
One life per run. Any lethal collision immediately ends the run (game over → restart).

## Game Objects & Entities
### Snake
- Starting length: **3 segments**, positioned near the arena center, heading right at start.
- Composed of a head tile plus body segments that trail along the head's previous path.
- States: alive (moving), dead (collision → triggers game over).

### Apple
- Single apple on the field at a time, drawn as a colorful pixel-art apple.
- Spawns on a random unoccupied tile; respawns immediately when eaten.
- Value: +1 score, +1 snake segment.

### Walls / boundaries
- The arena is a square bounded by a solid lethal outer wall — the snake's head touching it ends the run.
- No interior walls or maze obstacles — open bordered square.

### Enemy snakes
Not present. The only moving hazard is the player's own tail.

## Win / Lose Conditions
- **Lose (death):** snake head enters the outer wall, or any tile occupied by its own body.
- **Win:** none — the game is endless. The objective is a high score; the run ends only on death.

## Progression & Difficulty
- No level progression and no speed ramp; the single arena and constant speed are stable.
- The sole difficulty driver is snake length: as the tail grows it fills more of the arena, shrinking safe space and raising self-collision risk. The natural skill ceiling is filling the board.
- Scoring: +1 per apple; current score shown during play, best score persisted across sessions.

## World / Level Structure
- A single fixed, square, bordered arena for the whole game — no procedural generation, no multiple levels.
- Grid: **25 × 25 tiles** at **16 px** per tile, giving a 400 × 400 px playfield inside the border.

## Art & Visual Direction
- Perspective: top-down.
- Style: modern, colorful 2D pixel art.
- Elements: a readable colored snake (distinct head), a colorful apple, and a clearly delineated border wall on a dark or grassy background.
- Mood: friendly, clean, high-readability arcade. Palette: a small, vivid retro palette.

## Audio Direction
- **MVP: no audio.** Ships silent for the first playable.
- Deferred (stretch): SFX for eat/turn/death and an optional looping chiptune track.

## UI / UX
- **Title screen:** game name, "Press Enter/Space to start", and the persistent high score.
- **In-game HUD:** current score (and high score) shown unobtrusively at the top.
- **Game-over screen:** final score, high score (with a "New Best!" callout when beaten), and a restart prompt.
- **Pause overlay:** simple "Paused" state that freezes the game.
- **Feedback/juice:** brief visual pop/flash on eating, and a short death effect (snake flash) before the game-over screen.

## Scope
### MVP (first playable)
- Single bordered square arena, grid-step snake with 4-direction control.
- One apple at a time, +1 growth/score, constant speed, one life.
- Title, in-game HUD, game-over, and pause screens; persistent high score.
- Colorful pixel-art visuals. No audio.

### Later / Stretch
- Audio (SFX + chiptune music).
- Bonus / timed food worth extra points.
- Optional interior maze walls or selectable arenas.
- Speed-ramp difficulty mode.

### Non-Goals
- Multiplayer / two-player versus.
- Enemy or AI-controlled snakes.
- Procedurally generated levels.
- Mobile/touch controls (desktop keyboard only for now).

## Technical Constraints
- Engine: Godot (2D), project under `snaketaskmaster/`.
- Pixel-art rendering per project standard.
- Keyboard input; windowed desktop resolution scaled from the pixel-art playfield.
